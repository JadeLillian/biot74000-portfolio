## Sequence Analysis Tools

### BLAST (Basic Local Alignment Search Tool)

**Use example:** I have used BLAST to determine how conserved the phoP gene is in _Salmonella enterica_. I took notice of the query coverage and E-value. 

**Strengths / limitations:** 
BLAST allows you to find other matching sequences very quickly.
---

### UniProt

**Use example:** I used UniProt to determine the amino acid sequence of the human TP53 gene and the phoP gene in Salmonella enterica.

**Strengths / limitations:** One of UniProt’s strengths is that it uses a five-level scale for protein existence, which clearly defines how well the protein structure has been studied and verified. Uniprot also gives a very in-depth description of the protein's function in the organism.

---

## Databases & Data Retrieval

### GenBank

**Use example:** I used GenBank to look at the reference sequence of the BRCA1 gene in humans

**Strengths / limitations:** GenBank has links to a lot of papers that explore the function of the gene and its variants in the indicated organism.

### BLASTN

**Use example:** I have used BLASTN to compare the phoP gene sequence to other known gene sequences 

**Strengths / limitations:** BLASTN shows just how close the uploaded sequence matches sections of reference genomes. This is very helpful when determining which species contain this gene. 
---

## Data Visualization

### R + RStudio

**Use example:** I used RStudio to make bar charts from data collected from our capstone research project.

**Strengths / limitations:** RStudio is extremely useful in making charts from massive amounts of data, but it takes a bit of time to learn the R coding language.
